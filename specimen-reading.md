## Influences & References

<section class="reading-section">
<h3>Phenomenology & Perception</h3>

<div class="reading-entry">
  <p class="reading-title">The Visible and the Invisible</p>
  <p class="reading-author text-muted">Maurice Merleau-Ponty, 1964</p>
  <p class="reading-note epistemic-fact">Foundation for thinking about embodied perception. The chiasm.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-entry">
  <p class="reading-title">Francis Bacon: The Logic of Sensation</p>
  <p class="reading-author text-muted">Gilles Deleuze, 1981</p>
  <p class="reading-note epistemic-inference">Sensation vs. representation. The diagram. Useful for thinking about process.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-entry">
  <p class="reading-title">Art and Visual Perception</p>
  <p class="reading-author text-muted">Rudolf Arnheim, 1954</p>
  <p class="reading-note epistemic-speculation">Gestalt principles. Perhaps dated, but still generative for composition.</p>
</div>

</section>

<section class="reading-section">
<h3>Contemplative Traditions</h3>

<div class="reading-entry">
  <p class="reading-title">The Tibetan Book of the Dead</p>
  <p class="reading-author text-muted">trans. Thurman / Fremantle & Trungpa</p>
  <p class="reading-note temporal-timeless">Bardo states. Luminosity. The conditional nature of appearance.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-entry">
  <p class="reading-title">The Cloud of Unknowing</p>
  <p class="reading-author text-muted">Anonymous, 14th c.</p>
  <p class="reading-note affective-cool">Apophatic approach. Unknowing as method.</p>
</div>

</section>

<section class="reading-section">
<h3>Material & Process</h3>

<div class="reading-entry">
  <p class="reading-title">The Materials and Techniques of Medieval Painting</p>
  <p class="reading-author text-muted">Daniel V. Thompson, 1936</p>
  <p class="reading-note content-data">Practical. Pigment grinding. Binder chemistry. Still relevant.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-entry">
  <p class="reading-title">Color and Culture</p>
  <p class="reading-author text-muted">John Gage, 1993</p>
  <p class="reading-note content-reference">Comprehensive history. Useful for contextualizing chromatic choices.</p>
</div>

</section>
