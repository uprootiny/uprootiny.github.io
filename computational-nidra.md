<section class="work-description">

<h2>Concept</h2>

<p class="epistemic-fact">A functional e-commerce interface that sells intangible nothingness. Users can complete real financial transactions to purchase the void.</p>

<p class="epistemic-inference">The work inverts commercial logic: rather than exchanging money for goods, participants pay to "embrace the void"—receiving nothing tangible in return.</p>

<h2>Questions Raised</h2>

<ul class="question-list">
  <li class="epistemic-question">What do we consider valuable, and why?</li>
  <li class="epistemic-question">Is value inherent in objects, or a construct of perception?</li>
  <li class="epistemic-question">Can a financial transaction itself be a form of art?</li>
</ul>

<h2>Visual Design</h2>

<p>Minimalist dark interface. Black ground, white monospace text. Two pricing tiers presented as hoverable cards: <span class="content-data">€24</span> and <span class="content-data">€118</span>. Integrated Stripe payment form.</p>

<p class="text-muted">The stark presentation creates tension between artistic concept and real monetary exchange.</p>

<h2>Participation</h2>

<p class="affective-intense">This is not a simulation. Payments are processed. Nothing is delivered.</p>

<p class="epistemic-speculation">Each transaction becomes a record of someone choosing to value nothing—a distributed ledger of conceptual commitment.</p>

</section>

<section class="work-context">

<h2>Context</h2>

<p><span class="content-annotation">Note:</span> The title "Nidra" derives from Sanskrit, meaning sleep or the state between waking and dreaming. The void here is not nihilistic absence but potent emptiness—the ground from which form arises.</p>

<p class="temporal-timeless">Related traditions: Śūnyatā (emptiness) in Buddhist philosophy; apophatic theology; John Cage's silence.</p>

</section>
